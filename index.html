The Secret Behind Perfect Biryani

Biryani isn't just rice and meat—it's an art form perfected over centuries. At MYHOUSE, our chefs unlock the true secrets of this royal dish.

The Three Golden Rules

1. Perfect Rice

Aged basmati soaked for exactly 30 minutes, parboiled to 85% doneness. Never mushy, always separate.

2. Layering Magic

Meat gravy first, then rice, then saffron milk. Dum cooking seals flavors for 45 precise minutes.

3. Spice Balance

23 whole spices slow-fried, 7 aromatic waters, hand-ground masala. Perfection in every grain.

"Biryani is poetry on a plate" – Chef Rahman

Pro Tip: The Dum Technique

Seal the pot with dough to trap steam. This creates pressure cooking that infuses every rice grain with meat essence.

Time: 45 mins
Temp: 180°C
Seal: Wheat dough
Rest: 15 mins post-cook

The Art of Balancing Indian Spices

Indian food is not "spicy" by default – it is layered. The real magic lies in knowing which spice leads, which supports, and which quietly finishes the dish.

The Flavor Pyramid

  1. Base warmth: cumin, coriander, black pepper.
  2. Aromatic lift: cardamom, clove, cinnamon, bay leaf.
  3. Fresh finish: coriander leaves, mint, lemon juice.

Our Cloud Kitchen Approach

Every curry at MYHOUSE is built in layers. Whole spices are tempered first, powdered spices go in with the masala, and fresh herbs are added only at the end to avoid bitterness.

Tadka

Oil + whole spices to create the foundation.

Masala

Onion, ginger, garlic, tomatoes slowly cooked.

Finish

Fresh herbs and acidity to brighten the dish.

Why Kebabs Taste Better from a Cloud Kitchen

Without the distractions of a dining room, our kebab chefs can focus purely on marination time, grill temperature, and smoke control – the three pillars of a perfect kebab.

The 3-Hour Rule

At MYHOUSE, no kebab skewer touches the grill before a minimum of 3 hours of marination. For some cuts, we go up to 12 hours for maximum tenderness and flavor.

Consistency Through Tech

Sensor-based grills, digital timers, and recipe-locked systems ensure each kebab you order tastes the same – juicy, smoky, and perfectly charred at the edges.

How We Keep Delivery Food Restaurant-Fresh

The biggest fear with delivery food is soggy, cold, or separated dishes. Our entire kitchen flow is designed to fight exactly that.

Cook–Pack–Dispatch in 7 Minutes

For every live order, our system triggers three stations together: hot mains, breads, and condiments. The goal is simple – your food leaves the kitchen within 7 minutes of finishing on the stove.

Smart Packaging

  • Vented lids for fried items to stay crisp.
  • Separate compartments for curry, rice, and breads.
  • Heat-retaining liners for longer routes.

Live Temperature Checks

Riders confirm food temperature at pickup. Any box below our threshold is rejected and re-fired.

Desserts That Travel Well

Not every dessert loves delivery. Some melt, some collapse, and some lose their magic in minutes. Our dessert menu is designed specifically for travel – rich, stable, and still indulgent when it reaches you.

Our Cloud-Friendly Sweet Stars

  • Gulab Jamun: Soaks more syrup as it rests, actually improving with time.
  • Rasmalai: Packed chilled so the malai stays thick and creamy.
  • Shahi Tukda: Bread and rabri packed separately to avoid sogginess.

Packaging That Protects

We use deep, narrow containers for syrup-based sweets so they don’t move too much in transit, and double-sealed lids for anything with rabri or thickened milk.

Temperature Control

Cold desserts go in insulated bags with ice packs.

No Spills

Tight lids + tamper-evident seals for liquids.

Last Bite = First Bite

Designed so the final spoon tastes as good as the first.

Building the Perfect Cloud Kitchen Menu

A cloud kitchen menu has to do more than look good on paper. It needs to cook fast, travel well, and still feel special when it lands on your dining table.

Our Three-Filter Menu Design

  1. Operational fit: Can we cook it consistently at scale?
  2. Delivery strength: Will it survive a 30–40 minute ride?
  3. Guest delight: Does it feel “premium” enough for MYHOUSE?

Signature Categories

That’s why our menu leans on biryanis, rich curries, kebabs, and Indo-Chinese – all dishes that hold heat, keep texture, and stay flavorful even after travel.

Data-Driven Decisions

We regularly rotate underperforming dishes out and promote guest favorites, keeping the menu focused and high-performing.
